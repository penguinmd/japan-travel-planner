üìã General Tips

üö∂ Always wander off the path Everything is amazing, and the best part is seeing new things that are not crowded by tons of people.
üçΩÔ∏è Restaurant Reservations - Tabelog In Japan they use Tabelog for restaurant reviews and reservations (not Yelp or Google). If you really want to go to a specific restaurant, try to get a reservation a few days before. You cannot get same-day reservations often.
üëü Wear nice shoes, lots of walking Get a room with a bathtub - your body will thank you.
üéí Pack light Travel with a backpack to carry on the flight and a personal item. You'll be going up and down so many stairs that a roller-bag will probably suck.
ü™ô Getting rid of coins Go to a 7-11, buy something and throw all your coins in the slot. It will give you back change if you have more coins than the cost of the item.
üí¥ No tipping anywhere Tipping is considered disrespectful.
ü§´ Be quiet on trains People prefer silence.
üß¥ Always have hand sanitizer Many bathrooms do not have soap.
üì± GET GOOGLE TRANSLATE You can take pictures of menus, signs, or anything else to translate. You can also download the language so you can use it when you don't have service.

üöÑ Train Travel

üì± Download the Suica app The app is red with flowers. Set up your account and add it to your Apple Wallet. You can use Apple Pay to add money onto your Suica card which is used for trains and subways. It will show your remaining balance. You can also use your Suica funds at convenience stores like Apple Pay. Set this up during the flights there or before you leave.
üé´ Express Tickets & Reserved Seating Some trains have reserved seating and require an express ticket. You get into the station using your Suica but then need an express ticket. If you don't have a ticket yet, just get on board and buy a ticket from the attendant. Do not miss a train just because you don't have a ticket yet. Worst case you end up standing instead of sitting.
üó∫Ô∏è Google Maps > Apple Maps Travel by train the entire trip. Google Maps will tell you which platform and car to take. Google Maps is better than Apple Maps.

ü¶ä Fushimi Inari District

Torii Gates Shrine Hiking
Fushimi Inari Shrine Loved it! Uphill hike with tons of orange torii gates. Super busy with tourists, but there are paths you can go down that are less crowded. ‚ô®Ô∏è Kinosaki

One of my favorite places in Japan. Seriously, you have to go. It's an onsen town with seven public onsens that are gender separated. You could book a private onsen for ~$60. Did not want to leave. So calm and quiet. Beautiful scenery.

Nishimuraya Hotel

Spent more at this onsen hotel that had two of its own private onsens in the lobby with a sauna and cold plunge. Stayed two nights. This was a lifesaver after walking around Kyoto and Tokyo - our bodies were hurting. This town is quiet and sooo pretty. It's pricey, but you won't regret it. The chef's seasonal dinner was one of the best meals we had.

Recommendation: Do two nights at Nishimuraya Hotel and then move to another hotel in town for another night or two. Loved being able to relax and not run all over the place.
